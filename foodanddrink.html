Food & Drink

Restaurants

Did you know that there’s a restaurant in Taiwan that serves all-you-can-eat hot pot and another one that serves all-you-can-drink bubble tea? Well, judging by the look on your face, you didn’t. I’ve been to both of these restaurants multiple times, and I can say with certainty that these will be the best restaurants you EVER go to! Not only is there an all-you-can-eat hot pot for one of the restaurants (which is an automatic yes), there’s also ICE CREAM for dessert, which means you’ll be able to kill two birds with one stone! The other restaurant has an all-you-can-drink bubble tea, and the menu is filled with all sorts of types of noodles. My favourite is the beef noodle soup, but you can pick whatever you want, because it’s not the meal that’s important, it’s the BUBBLE TEA!

Groceries

Of course, there’s your typical supermarket in Taiwan, but there are also supermarkets that are set in specific streets. Along some alleys, you can find a trail of shops or stands that sell a variety of different items, ranging from fruits to clothing. These supermarkets are similar to the night markets, but they’re open in the day, and most of them sell groceries instead of fast food.

Night Market

You’ve probably never been to a night market before, but let me tell you, they are AMAZING! These markets are open at night, and most of them sell snacks, fast food, clothing, and bubble tea. The environment is usually pretty loud and crowded, but that’s part of the reason why I like it so much. You can walk around while eating and exploring the area of the night market because they’re only open during the night.
