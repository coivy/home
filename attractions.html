Taipei 101

Taipei 101 was formerly known as the Taipei World Financial Center, but that’s not important. You can go from the 5th to the 89th floor in only 37 seconds! There are 101 floors, hence the name and indoor observatories are available to the public. On the lower floors of the building, there is a “luxury atrium shopping mall”, according to Britannica. In 2011, Taipei 101 got a Platinum rating, becoming the tallest AND largest green building in the world. This building is an iconic landmark in Taiwan, and I need to go there someday, preferably soon (as in now)!

Trampoline Parks

I could spend an entire day at a trampoline park because that’s how fun it is! According to NASA, trampolining benefits are 68% more efficient than jogging. I’ve been to numerous trampoline parks in Taiwan, and there are just so many things to do: gymnastics, jumping on the Olympic trampolines, obstacle courses, eating pizza, you name it, the park probably has it. Did you know, when you jump on a trampoline, you build strength in every cell of your body? One more reason to let me go to Taiwan (the reason is to be healthier if you didn’t get that already)!

Epic Views

There are a lot of EPIC views in Taiwan. One of the most popular EPIC views is Sun Moon Lake. It’s the largest body of water in Taiwan, and if I go there, I’m planning on staying for at least a week, because there are so many things to do there. This attraction includes multiple other attractions within and around the lake. There are some bikeways, cultural villages, temples, piers and a “Green Tunnel” that I want to visit.

Amusement Parks

Although there aren’t any Disneylands in Taiwan, there are other non-ripoff amusement parks just waiting for me to explore! I’d just like to point out that in all of my time in Taiwan, I literally cannot remember a single time we went to an amusement park. This could be my chance to relive my childhood again! For example, Lihpao Land has the biggest Ferris Wheel in Taiwan, along with a go-kart course, and numerous roller coasters. This amusement park has the first roller coaster with a 90-degree drop and a tilt coaster! Additional information can be found on Wikipedia.

Neighbouring Countries

Taiwan has a lot of neighbouring countries that aren’t as fun as Taiwan, but still pretty good. I’ve been on a guided trip to Korea, and it was really fun, as you already know. However, I have yet to go to Japan, and I also want to go back to Korea to experience more fun attractions. During the guided tour of Korea, I went to an indoor ice rink, but it wasn’t just a plain ice rink; there was a dog sledding ride!
